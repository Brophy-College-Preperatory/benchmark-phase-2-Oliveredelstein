    • Early Days (Pre-20th Century)

    McGill University Hockey Club (1877): Often credited as one of the first organized hockey teams, the McGill University Hockey Club in Montreal helped formalize the sport by creating early rules, which became the basis for modern hockey. Montreal Victorias: One of the earliest teams to play in Montreal, the Victorias were a dominant force and part of the early Amateur Hockey Association of Canada (AHAC). Ottawa Hockey Club (1893): Known later as the Ottawa Senators, this team was among the first to compete for the Stanley Cup, which was introduced in 1893.

    The Formation of the NHL (1917)

    The National Hockey League (NHL) was established in 1917 with original teams like the Montreal Canadiens, Toronto Arenas (which became the Maple Leafs), Ottawa Senators, and Montreal Wanderers. Over time, teams like the Quebec Bulldogs and Hamilton Tigers joined, although these teams didn't last. The NHL grew to dominate professional hockey in North America.

    The Original Six Era (1942–1967)

    From 1942 until 1967, the NHL consisted of six teams, known as the "Original Six": Montreal Canadiens: The most successful team in NHL history, with numerous Stanley Cup titles. Toronto Maple Leafs: Another Canadian powerhouse with multiple championships, although they faced a long Cup drought after 1967. Detroit Red Wings: Known for players like Gordie Howe, they were one of the top American teams. Boston Bruins: One of the oldest American teams, boasting legends like Bobby Orr. New York Rangers: New York's premier team with a dedicated fan base. Chicago Blackhawks: Known for iconic players and later, for success in the 2010s.

    Expansion Era (1967–Present)

    1967 Expansion: The NHL doubled in size, adding six teams: the Los Angeles Kings, Minnesota North Stars (now Dallas Stars), Philadelphia Flyers, Pittsburgh Penguins, St. Louis Blues, and California Seals (which became the Cleveland Barons and later merged with Minnesota). Further Expansion: The league continued to grow, adding teams such as the Buffalo Sabres, Vancouver Canucks, New York Islanders, and Edmonton Oilers. Modern NHL: Today, the league has 32 teams, including recent additions like the Vegas Golden Knights (2017) and the Seattle Kraken (2021).
